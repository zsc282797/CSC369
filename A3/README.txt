2. Comparison: 
Random did pretty well but not always stable, sometimes it would get very close to the optimal solution but sometimes it will be significantly lower than other. 
This unstable behavior works but not predictable. 

FIFO is straight forward and very easy to implement. The performace of hit rate is not as good as others since it does not consider the "running " aspect of program (The sequence of access). It usually has the lowest hit rate becuase of this. (Doing pretty well on block and simpleloop since memory is accessed in serial sequence).

CLOCK gives a second chance to the page before eviting and it gives very similar results as LRU. This is well expected since they use close ideas as counting the age of a page. They generally perform betterh than FIFO but sometimes can be out-performed by rand (very rare)

OPT is the cheating method where we have the ability to see into all the future accesses. This serves as the performance bound. As we can see, no other algorithm would perform better than OPT. (Very slow though due to reading the whole tracing file). Not practical
